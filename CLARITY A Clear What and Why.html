Northstar Point Two
Focus — Life Stage Strategy
The Five-Year Lens — what this chapter is about and what we're focused on.
🎯
What is this five-year chapter of your life actually about?
If you close your eyes and picture yourself five years from today — what does your life look like at its best?
Not just financially. Where are you living? What are you doing with your time? Who are you spending it with? How do you feel?
What has to be true in 5 years for you to feel like this chapter of your life was a success?
These don't all have to be financial. Think about health, relationships, experiences, career, family — what are the things that would make you say "yes, that was exactly what it needed to be"?
What is this five-year chapter NOT about?
Just as important as knowing what you want is knowing what you're consciously choosing not to prioritize. What are you willing to say no to for now?

Northstar Point Three
Fit — Investment Strategy
An investment strategy designed to fit you and no one else.
📊
How does money feel? — Your relationship with investing
When markets drop significantly — say 20-30% — what do you actually do? Not what you should do. What do you actually do?
There's no wrong answer. We need to build a strategy that fits who you actually are, not who you think you should be as an investor.
How do you feel about your current investment strategy — does it fit you, or does something feel off?
Sometimes the strategy is right on paper but wrong for the person. Be honest about whether your current approach helps you sleep at night.
What rate of return do you need your investments to generate to achieve your five-year goals?
Are there any investments, sectors, or strategies you want to avoid — or specifically explore?
In five years, what would a "win" look like for your investment portfolio?
Not a number necessarily — how do you want to feel about your investments in five years?

Northstar Point Four
Stability — Risk Management
Creating stability when life is uncertain.
🛡️
What could go wrong? — Protecting what matters most
If something happened to you tomorrow — illness, disability, or death — what would be the biggest financial impact on your family?
Not a pleasant thought, but an important one. The goal is to make sure the plan survives even when life doesn't go as planned.
Do you feel confident that if you couldn't work for 6-12 months, your family would be financially stable?
1 — Not at all confident10 — Completely protected
What keeps you up at night financially?
Over the next 5 years, what risks or uncertainties concern you most?
Have there been any major life changes since we last reviewed your protection — health, employment, dependants, assets?

Northstar Point Five
Legacy — Estate Strategy
Legacy is not what you owned… it is the emotional, relational, financial, and moral footprint you leave behind.
🌟
What do you want to leave behind? — Thinking about the long arc
When you're gone, how do you want the people who mattered most to you to remember you — and what do you want to have passed on to them?
This isn't just about money. Think about values, beliefs, experiences, wisdom, and yes — financial assets. What's the legacy you want to leave?
Is there something you want to give — to family, a cause, or a community — while you're still alive to see it happen?
Do your legal documents — will, POA, health directive — reflect your current wishes and family situation?
Is there anyone in your family whose financial future worries you — someone you feel responsible for or want to protect?
Aging parents, a child with special needs, a sibling in difficulty, a grandchild you want to set up for success.
In five years, what is one thing you want to have done — financially or otherwise — that would make you feel proud of how you handled this chapter?
This is the closing question. What's the one thing?
