The FIT Conversation — Like a Glove
13 questions · 30 min
The purpose of this conversation
To clearly understand what investing means to you — how you experience volatility — and what kind of portfolio allows you to stay invested with confidence and peace of mind.

We are not trying to maximize returns.
We are trying to maximize the chance that you stay invested comfortably, for the long term.
💭
What investing means to you
Questions 1–3 · Foundation
1What does investing actually represent to you?
Meaning & Values
Not what it should represent — what does it actually mean in your life? Security? Freedom? Something you built? A responsibility you carry?
2What does a good investment experience feel like to you?
Emotional Fit
Not a good return — a good experience. How do you want to feel about your money day to day?
3Have you had a negative investment experience — a significant loss, a bad advisor relationship, something that created real caution?
Past Experience
This shapes everything. We need to know what you're carrying into this conversation before we go any further.
📉
How you respond to volatility
Questions 4–6 · The core of the conversation
4When markets fall significantly — say 20 to 30% in a year — what happens inside you? What do you actually do?
Behavioral Tolerance
Not what you think you should do. Not what a calm, rational investor would do. What do you actually do — and feel?
5Which feels worse: watching your portfolio lose significant value — or watching from the sidelines while markets recover without you?
Loss vs Regret
There's no right answer. This is about what actually causes you more pain. Both are real risks — this tells us which one to protect against first.
6Do you see market volatility as risk to be avoided — or as opportunity to be used?
Market Psychology
Some people see a 20% drop and feel sick. Some see it and want to buy more. Both are valid — but they lead to completely different portfolio designs.
📅
Your financial foundation & time horizon
Questions 7–9 · Capacity — not just tolerance
7When do you expect to start drawing on this money — and how much will you need from it each year?
Time Horizon & Need
This is the most practical question in the conversation. Time horizon changes everything. Someone who doesn't need this money for 20 years can afford a very different portfolio than someone drawing on it in 3.
8If your portfolio dropped 25% tomorrow and stayed down for two full years — would your financial life remain stable? Could you afford to wait it out?
Risk Capacity
This is the difference between what you're willing to accept emotionally and what you can actually afford financially. Both matter. The lower of the two drives the strategy.
9How stable is your income right now — and do you expect that to change in the next few years?
Income Stability
Financial stability on the income side affects how much risk the portfolio side can carry. A stable income is a cushion. An unstable one means the portfolio may need to work harder as a safety net.
🔭
How you want to engage with your investments
Questions 10–13 · Behavioral design
10Do you prefer to understand and be involved in your investments — or to delegate completely and trust the process?
Engagement Style
There's no wrong answer. This tells us how much education, communication, and involvement to build into the relationship.
11Does financial news — market headlines, interest rate announcements, recession talk — affect your peace of mind?
News Sensitivity
Some people can tune it out completely. Others find it hard to ignore. The answer changes how we need to communicate during turbulent periods.
12Should your core portfolio feel more like a pension — steady, structured, long-term, something you don't think about often — or more growth-oriented, with more movement in exchange for higher long-term upside?
Portfolio Feel
This is the character question. How should your money feel to live with?
13In five years, what would a win look like for your investment portfolio — not just in returns, but in experience?
Success Definition
This is the closing question. Not what do you want it to earn — how do you want it to have felt? What is the experience of success for you?

Two-Dimension Scoring — Tolerance & Capacity
ℹ️The FIT profile is determined by the lower of two scores: emotional tolerance and financial capacity. A client can have high capacity but low tolerance — they get the conservative profile. A client can have high tolerance but low capacity — same result. Both dimensions must align before increasing risk. This is the CIRO-aligned approach.
